#  Exercise 7: Onshape CAD Self Study



##  Overview
This exercise served as an introduction to Computer-Aided Design (CAD) utilizing Onshape's cloud-based platform. The goal was to build a strong foundational understanding of parametric modeling, sketch creation, and core part design workflows central to digital fabrication and engineering.

To achieve this, the practical exercise involved completing three foundational Onshape training modules:

- Introduction to Parametric Feature-Based CAD
- Introduction to Sketching
- Introduction to Part Studios


---


###  Course 1 – Introduction to Parametric Feature-Based CAD

This module introduced the core principles of parametric and feature-based modeling. I learned how 3D models are constructed using editable dimensions, geometric constraints, and a chronological feature history. This design approach ensures that any modifications can be made efficiently by adjusting existing parameters, eliminating the need to rebuild parts from scratch.


###  Course 2 – Introduction to Sketching

This module focused on creating the precise 2D sketches that serve as the foundation for 3D geometry. I gained hands-on experience using sketching tools, applying dimensions, and utilizing geometric constraints to build fully defined sketches. Mastering these techniques ensures that designs maintain structural accuracy and adapt predictably when modifications are made.


###  Course 3 – Introduction to Part Studios

This module explored Onshape’s Part Studio environment, demonstrating how to effectively transform 2D sketches into complex, three-dimensional geometry. I developed a practical understanding of essential modeling features—including Extrude, Revolve, Fillet, and Chamfer—and learned how to utilize these tools to create, modify, and refine production-ready CAD models.


###  Certification Milestone

The Onshape training path was successfully completed, validating proficiency across the required core modeling modules.

##  Conclusion

Completing this independent study offered a highly practical introduction to cloud-based CAD and modern digital design workflows. Working through the Onshape modules bridged the gap between abstract concepts and actual design execution, providing a clear understanding of how parametric constraints, sketching, and 3D feature creation dictate physical geometry.

Ultimately, this training shifted my perspective from simply looking at a finished object to understanding how digital models are systematically built for real-world production. Developing these core modeling skills establishes a critical technical foundation that will be directly applicable to upcoming Digital Design and Fabrication projects involving 3D printing, laser cutting, and CNC machining.
